Decades

Exploring the evolution of website design trends across the 90s, 2000s, 2010s, and 2020s.

The 90s

Web design in the 90s was characterized by the use of HTML and CSS. Websites were simple and static, with little to no interactivity. The most popular websites of the time were Yahoo, AOL, and eBay.

The 2000s

Web design in the 2000s saw the rise of Flash and JavaScript, which allowed for more interactivity and animation. Websites became more dynamic and visually appealing, with the introduction of multimedia content such as videos and music.

The 2010s

Web design in the 2010s was characterized by the widespread use of responsive design, which allowed websites to adapt to different screen sizes and devices. This decade also saw the rise of social media and mobile browsing, leading to the development of mobile-first design principles.

The 2020s

Web design in the 2020s is characterized by the use of modern web technologies such as HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript frameworks like React and Angular. Websites are more interactive and visually appealing, with a focus on user experience and accessibility.
