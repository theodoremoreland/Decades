Exploring website design trends across four decades

The 90s

Characterized by:

  • Bevel and emboss effects
  • Animated GIFs
  • Animated cursor
  • Grainy images
  • Colored icons
  • Cheesy colored box/drop shadows and borders
  • Bulleted lists
  • Unstyled links (except for color - red or blue)
  • Unstyled forms and fields
  • Unstyled scrollbars
  • Welcome text
  • Jarring color palette

The 2000s

Web design in the 2000s made liberal use of acute gradients and background images, but still featured many of the unstyled HTML elements from the 90s.

The 2010s

Web design in the 2010s upped the resolution of images (and corresponding screens), making them more appropriate for larger sizes. Headers and footers were also enlarged. Designs were often minimalistic with grid based layouts. Responsive designs were becoming the norm. One pay layouts were trending.

The 2020s

Web design in the 2020s is the age of dynamic presentations. Things such as text or images that appear as the user scrolls have become fairly common. Responsive designs have become the norm.
