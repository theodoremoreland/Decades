Decades

Exploring the evolution of website design trends across the last four decades.

Introduction

Good web design requires attention to detail, detail that is often too subtle to recognize without practice and study. In my pursuit to bring these subtleties to light, I explore how web design has evolved over time. In juxtaposing the design trends of the last four decades, I hope to unravel patterns in making good web designs.

Objective

The objective of this project is to compare design trends across the last four decades, but doing so with a particular focus. In an effort to establish clear contrast without overburdening myself on technical details (e.g. Flash, JavaScript, etc.), I will focus on a handful of visual dimensions.

The dimensions I will be focusing on are as follows:

  • Color
  • Typography
  • Layout
  • Imagery
  • Interative elements

The 90s

Web design in the 90s used a lot of bulleted lists, bevel and emboss effects, crude shadows and borders, and use of color that, by today's standards, do not complement each other well.

The 2000s

Web design in the 2000s made liberal use of acute gradients and background images, but still featured many of the unstyled HTML elements from the 90s.

The 2010s

Web design in the 2010s upped the resolution of images (and corresponding screens), making them more appropriate for larger sizes. Headers and footers were also enlarged. Designs were often more minimalistic with layouts reminiscent of newspapers and magazines.

The 2020s

Web design in the 2020s is the age of dynamic presentations. Things such as text or images that appear as the user scrolls have become fairly common.
