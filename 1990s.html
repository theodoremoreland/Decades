The 90s

Web design in the 90s used a lot of bulleted lists, bevel and emboss effects, crude shadows and borders, and use of color that, by today's standards, do not complement each other well.

Color

Color in the 90s was often jarring, with a lot of bright, saturated colors that did not complement each other well. The use of color was often inconsistent and did not follow any particular pattern.

Typography

Typography in the 90s was often unstyled, with the exception of color. Text was often left unstyled, with the default font and size being used. Links were often styled with color, but nothing else.

Layout

Layout in the 90s was often unstructured, with a lot of bulleted lists and unstyled forms and fields. The use of tables was common, and the use of frames was also common.

Imagery

Imagery in the 90s was often grainy, with the use of animated GIFs and colored icons. The use of images was often inconsistent and did not follow any particular pattern.

Interactive Elements

Interactive elements in the 90s were often unstyled, with the exception of color. Links were often styled with color, but nothing else. Forms and fields were often unstyled, and scrollbars were often unstyled.
