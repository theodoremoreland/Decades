The 2000s

Web design in the 2000s was characterized by the use of gradients, drop shadows, and rounded corners. The use of color was often more subdued than in the 90s, and typography was often more stylized.

Color

Color in the 2000s was often more subdued than in the 90s, with a lot of pastel colors being used. The use of color was often more consistent, with a particular color palette being used throughout the website.

Typography

Typography in the 2000s was often more stylized than in the 90s, with the use of custom fonts and text effects being more common. Links were often styled with color, and text was often given a drop shadow or emboss effect.

Layout

Layout in the 2000s was often more complex than in the 90s, with the use of gradients, drop shadows, and rounded corners being more common. The use of background images was also more common, often distractingly so.

Imagery

Imagery in the 2000s was often low resolution, with the use of colored, low resolution icons being common. The use of grainy images was also common, and the use of background images was often distractingly so.

Interactive elements

Interactive elements in the 2000s were often unstyled, with the exception of color. Links were often styled with color, and forms and fields were often left unstyled.
